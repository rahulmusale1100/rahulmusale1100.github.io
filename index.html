Research Projects & Experience

  • Extragalactic Compact Radio Sources - Description: Our work mainly focuses on Investigating asymmetries in Compact Steep Spectrum (CSS) and Peaked Spectrum (PS) Sources. The majority of these sources are likely to be young sources, advancing outwards through a dense inhomogeneous environment. By evaluating symmetry parameters and using HI column densities, We are investigating whether orientation-based projection effects or intrinsic asymmetries dominate the asymmetric nature of these sources.
  • Magnetic Inclination Angle of the Pulsar - Description: We are investigating whether the magnetic inclination angle of the Pulsar affect the properties of Pulsar emission and by looking at its magnetic inclination angle and pulse profile shape, we intend to find whether there is any relation between them.
  • Radio Image Analysis Using CASA - Description: In this project, I have gone through different aspects of radio image synthesis like cross correlators, calibrations (flux, phase, bandpass, etc), deconvolution, and error recognition. During this project, I worked on the raw radio interferometer data from the Very Large Array (VLA) and Atacama Large Millimeter/Submillimeter Array (ALMA) for image synthesis using CASA. For VLA data, I made HI radio images of the Spiral galaxy NGC 5921 and 3C 75 in the cluster of the galaxies Abell 400 at 1.4 GHz Frequency.
